ABOUT ME

Computer science became my interest and I made it the subject of my studies because I was first passionate about the creative potential that comes with it. For me, mastering IT means mastering a tool that allows me to be able to create solutions or simply be creative in its pure sense. I also really enjoy working with UI/UX, making applications visually satisfying, smart and efficient. On the other hand, because the video game industry pushes these aspects to the maximum, I particularly care about it. And then I realised that in general, working in development by looking for the best solutions and solve some technical challenges was really my main motivation.

In fact, there are several reasons why I am so interested in doing this. One of them being simply that I don't feel particularly attached to a country, I prefer the idea of being a European citizen or even a citizen of the world. On another side, I believe it is a very rewarding experience to have if you feel like doing it. I am convinced that it will bring me a lot in terms of cultural knowledge, openness and understanding of the people I would meet. First, I was not targeting a particular country, I was just thinking of any western and English speaking country. Today, I know that I want to live this adventure in Germany and start it as soon as possible.

By meeting people and making friends through the internet from many places in the world, I have gathered a lot of pros and cons for moving to different countries. And that's how I came to the conclusion that: the most interesting place for me would be Germany. Germany being in Europe, it is also a good point for me as I will not be too far from my French family if I have to visit them a few times. Also, by the time I drew this conclusion, I was still not really able to speak a word in German so I decided to immediately start learning it on a daily basis as I am convinced that it is my best choice. Although, I have seen and some German friends have told me that I could do it by speaking only English at first. It is probably correct but I have to learn German for myself. I want to understand people better and because I am very aware of what this brings in terms of culture and understanding in general, I cannot ignore it and am very willing to learn it and be fluent.

    I started this small project primarily to measure my advanced knowledge and coding skills from scratch about making some sort of game engine. I wanted to do something simple and achievable. I used TypeScript because I like the endless possibilities by mixing paradygms in JavaScript and TypeScript was a good solution to get a better view of the project from a technical side. It also gave me the possibility to share the project on a web page which is very nice to present a small demo. And so, I developed this little game demo from scratch. All the animations are defined with some internal properties that I have defined mainly with an Object Oriented paradygm, the level is defined by a JSON file containing an array with each of the blocks with their nature and their displayed image. The mechanisms of gravity and collision were also created from scratch. Each entity is actually an HTML node wrapped in more complex and appropriate objects and controlled by the game.

    I knew from the start that using TypeScript / JavaScript was not an easy solution to build a stable game and so it would require a lot of optimizations. Although on this part I think I did a great job of making it effective but at the same time if I were to make it a full game it would start to get complicated with this kind of technology anyways. It was fun, and I quite like the result though. I also thought about editing the SVG image files directly in the game to add a little customization to the character. Unfortunately, while SVG is quite handy for this because it is as easy to dynamically edit as the HTML I was using, some web browser security principles do not allow these kinds of edits. And it would have also turned off most of the preloading optimizations from the browser. On the other hand, with a custom browser like an app, I could work around this problem in order to make it work. And obviously, it's far from a serious project with a tailor-made desktop application capable of communicating with video drivers directly and with a more appropriate coding language able to manage used memory more precisly etc.

    A modeling project

    Self-taught using Blender

    Something I really wanted to take a look at for a while was 3D modeling. In fact, wasn't a stranger to Blender and 3D modeling before. During my studies at three occasions I had courses that gotten me really interested about 3D. The first during my DUT degree was a very short introduction to Maya 3DS Max: a premium 3D software well known in the field. Then during my next years at the University in two occasions I got a course about 3D technologies, algorithms and all what's behind software such as Blender & 3DS Max as well as how GPUs are rendering images and 3D. All this was mostly on the technical side however, it built a very useful background knowledge for me.

    After watching some videos with people modeling characters in Blender. I got a good idea of what workflow I would have to use. I decided to start this project and use a lot of my passion for character design with me and this whole topic of "bringing my creations to life"! I took it slowly. Then, because of my background knowledge I chose to approach modeling by focusing on topology rather than sculpting it with an approach emulating clay sculpting. This way I had a direct control over the polygon amount of my meshes and I could take care of making it coherent and usable later for the animation part of the project. At the same time I have really been aiming to make the model usable in game engines, especially in Unity. For that, I took care of respecting constraints from the mesh polygon complexity to the animation rig and skeleton. After around three weeks of work on that project, I managed to get the result you have been looking at. My model is fully rigged have even been ported to Unity. It is successfully integrated in Virtual Reality games as an avatar for games such as VRChat and as a VRM that can be used in virtual webcam applications and animate the model over your facial movements and expressions.

    I really loved working on this project. From the beginning to the end. I learned a lot and it has been satisfying at every steps, creating a new character, giving him the ability to move, putting him into a game... I really enjoyed how many pieces of knowledge I had to put together for this project. I am really looking forward to do some other modeling projects in the future. With the model fully implemented in Unity that also makes me able to eventually is it as an asset for 3D game projects.

    I was asked to build a wiki in order to share knowledge between researchers at the INRAE research institute and with all stakeholders. I had to adapt the wiki to the graphic charter of the research institute, and of the team commissioning the wiki. I had to take care of MediaWiki and all the server setup involved, including the REST subservers I needed to define in order to make the content dynamic and connect the visual editor UI. And so I built a wiki called Wiki-PEPS. This my first big project in terms of development and for a real customer and it was a big one. Especially when dealing with REST services like REST-Base and the whole ecosystem that I had to put in place to make it work. It was a great experience with deep configurations on Linux servers and these new tools making all the REST directives available. I learned on my own how to handle it with limited documentation and only available in English for the most part.

    Nowadays, the wiki is no longer hosted on a public host. The screenshot here depicts a slightly broken version of the wiki that was retrieved through the Internet archive.

      One of the great tools in an app I developed for a company I interned in was a PowerPoint-like visual editor.       This app is now part of a larger app used internally at the company I worked for. They were really happy with the whole project, the main app should have a bright future.
